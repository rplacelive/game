Privacy Terms:

We do not sell or offer your personal information to any third parties. We do however, for the site's functionality, collect the following information:

IP addresses, session identifiers, and connection metadata

We log IP addresses, session IDs, connection times, and related metadata. This data is used to enforce cooldowns, rate limits, anti-bot measures, and moderation actions. IPs and session data may be retained across sessions and associated with your user ID to prevent ban evasion and abuse.

User IDs, usernames, and user info

When you connect to an rplace.live canvas, a persistent user ID may be assigned. This ID is used to associate your username, sessions, IP addresses, activity, and moderation history. Persistent user data is stored invisibly, and may only be removable upon request.

Browser, device, and fingerprinting data

We collect information such as user agent strings, browser characteristics, and other fingerprint-like signals. This is primarily used to strengthen moderation and ban enforcement, especially to detect attempts to bypass restrictions.

Activity metadata

We log gameplay and site activity such as:
  • Session duration and play time
  • Pixels placed and interactions performed
  • Chat participation and related timestamps
This data is currently used mainly for moderation, abuse prevention, and internal analysis, but may also be used in the future for analytics, feature development, or balancing decisions.

Chat messages and pixel placements

By submitting chat messages, placing pixels, or otherwise contributing content, you understand that this content is stored persistently. Removal is not guaranteed and generally only occurs upon request or moderation action.

Moderation and enforcement

All collected data may be cross-referenced to a reasonable extent to detect abuse, enforce bans, and prevent circumvention. If you are banned, attempts to evade that ban using alternate accounts, devices, or connections may be detected using this data.

Science Telemetrics

We occasionally collect non-identifying information about your hardware or behavior in the game for research purposes, such as developing new features or technologies to improve the experience of everyone in the game.
